Helping Hand

In collaboration with the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab in Chicago, IL, the Helping Hand was created as an assistive gripping device for users with limited hand function due to spinal cord injuries. As part of a team of four Northwestern engineering students, I created a high-fidelity prototype for one specific user, which enabled him to walk with crutches, operate a vehicle, and eat independently.

SipLine

In collaboration with the Shirley Ryan AbilityLab in Chicago, IL, the SipLine was created as a call method for patients who are unable to move their limbs. Rather than pressing a button as most patients do to alert a nurse that they are in need of attention, users of the SipLine blow into a tube attached to their bed and positioned near the mouth. The change in air pressure sets off a notification at the nurses' station.
